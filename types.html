1. Direct Plagiarism

Direct plagiarism occurs when someone copies another person's work word-for-word without citation.

2. Self-Plagiarism

Self-plagiarism involves reusing one's own previously published work in a new piece without acknowledgment.

3. Mosaic Plagiarism

Mosaic plagiarism, also known as patchwork plagiarism, occurs when a writer combines pieces of various sources and presents them as their own.

4. Accidental Plagiarism

Accidental plagiarism happens when a writer neglects to cite sources or misquotes them without intent to deceive.

5. Paraphrasing Plagiarism

Paraphrasing plagiarism occurs when someone rewrites another person's ideas in their own words without proper citation.

6. Source-Based Plagiarism

This type occurs when the source of the material is not acknowledged, regardless of how much of the original text is changed.
